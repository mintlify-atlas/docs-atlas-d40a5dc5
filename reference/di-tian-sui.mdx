**Core contribution**: 旺衰判断、气势流通 — strength assessment and Qi flow.

Where 穷通宝典 asks "what element does this climate need?", 滴天髓 asks "how strong is the day master, and does the chart's energy move or stagnate?" The text provides both the three-dimension framework for measuring day-master strength and the principles for evaluating whether elemental Qi circulates freely or is blocked.

## Three dimensions of strength

滴天髓 judges day-master (日主) strength through three conditions. All three should be evaluated, but they are not equal.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="得令 (Dé Lìng) — Month command">
    The most important of the three conditions.

    **Definition**: the day master is in a favorable state within the month branch (月令). Specifically, the day master's element is at 长生 (birth), 帝旺 (peak), or 墓库 (storage/grave) in the month branch according to the twelve-stage cycle (十二长生).

    **Why it dominates**: the month branch is the seasonal ruler of the chart. Whatever element governs the month governs the climate. A day master that receives seasonal support from the month branch has the most powerful form of backing — environmental, not just structural.

    A day master that does not 得令 begins from a position of climatic weakness, regardless of stem or branch support elsewhere.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="得地 (Dé Dì) — Branch roots">
    **Definition**: the day master has roots (根) in one or more of the other three branch positions (year, day, or hour branch). A branch "root" means the day master's element appears at 长生, 帝旺, or 墓库 in that branch.

    **Effect**: branch roots provide structural grounding. Even when the month does not support the day master, strong roots in other branches can partially compensate.

    The depth of rooting matters: 帝旺 root is the strongest, 长生 is moderate, 墓库 is weak and conditional (the element is stored, not active, and needs a specific trigger to release).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="得势 (Dé Shì) — Stem support">
    **Definition**: the heavenly stems contain elements that directly support the day master — either 比劫 (parallel and rob-wealth, same element as day master) that strengthen it, or 印星 (seal) that generates it.

    **Effect**: stem support represents active, visible allies. It is the most surface-level form of strength — powerful but also the most easily neutralized by opposing stems.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  三得之中，得令最为重要 — among the three conditions, 得令 (month command) is the most important. A day master that 得令 is considered strong even with limited branch roots or stem support. A day master that does not 得令 needs both 得地 and 得势 to be considered genuinely strong.
</Note>

## Qi flow (气势流通)

Strength assessment tells you how strong the day master is. Qi flow analysis tells you whether the chart's energy moves constructively or is locked in conflict.

### The flow principle

> 命局喜五行流通，不喜阻滞。
>
> *A chart favors the free circulation of the five elements; it disfavors stagnation.*

The ideal chart has a generation chain (相生链) where each element feeds the next: Metal → Water → Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal. When energy flows along a continuous generation sequence, the chart's potential expresses smoothly across multiple domains.

**Example of smooth flow**: a chart where Metal generates Water, Water generates Wood, and Wood generates Fire has a three-element generation chain. The energy flows in one direction; each element receives and passes on without conflict.

### Blockage and mediation

When two elements in a chart are in direct clash or restraint (相克), the flow is blocked. The classic solution is a **mediation element** (通关之物): an element that generates both combatants, converting confrontation into a generation sequence.

> 若某五行过旺或过弱，造成堵塞，则需通关之物。如金木相战，取水通关。
>
> *If any element is excessively strong or weak, creating blockage, a mediating element is required. Example: when Metal and Wood are in conflict, Water mediates.*

| Conflict | Mediating element | Logic |
|---|---|---|
| Metal vs. Wood | Water | Metal generates Water; Water generates Wood — confrontation becomes generation |
| Wood vs. Earth | Fire | Wood generates Fire; Fire generates Earth |
| Earth vs. Water | Metal | Earth generates Metal; Metal generates Water |
| Water vs. Fire | Wood | Water generates Wood; Wood generates Fire |
| Fire vs. Metal | Earth | Fire generates Earth; Earth generates Metal |

When the mediating element is present and strong, the conflict dissolves into flow. When it is absent, the clash remains and the chart's energy is consumed by internal friction.

### The key maxim

> 旺极宜泄不宜克，衰极宜扶不宜帮。
>
> *When an element is at peak strength, drain it (via generation output) rather than attack it directly. When an element is at extreme weakness, support it — but do not keep feeding an element that has already collapsed into following the dominant force.*

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Extremely strong element: drain, do not attack">
    A dominant element at peak strength is like a river in flood. Attacking it (with a restraining element) creates violent resistance — the restraining element is overwhelmed and the conflict damages the chart. The better approach is to drain the excess through the element it generates (泄秀, "drain the elegance").

    **Example**: an extremely strong Wood day master is better served by Fire (Wood generates Fire, draining the excess) than by Metal (Metal restrains Wood, but weak Metal is shattered by strong Wood).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Extremely weak element: the following (从) consideration">
    When a day master is so weak it has no root, no seasonal support, and is surrounded entirely by opposing or draining elements, conventional "support" is counterproductive. Introducing the day master's own element or its generating element disrupts the dominant flow without being strong enough to reverse it.

    In this state — 衰极 (extreme weakness) — the chart may qualify as a "following pattern" (从格): the day master abandons resistance and follows the dominant force. See [渊海子平](/reference/yuan-hai-zi-ping) for the full classification of following patterns.

    <Warning>
      Determining whether a chart is a genuine 从格 or simply a weak-but-conventional chart is one of the most contested judgment calls in classical Four Pillars analysis. 滴天髓 supplies the conceptual framework; chart-specific judgment requires synthesizing all three strength conditions.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Three-path framework: Heaven, Earth, Human

滴天髓 also introduces a three-path framework (天道、地道、人道) for understanding the chart's layers:

| Path | Pillar | Nature | Governs |
|---|---|---|---|
| 天道 (Heaven's Way) | Heavenly stems (天干) | Active, yang | External expression and visible behavior |
| 地道 (Earth's Way) | Earthly branches (地支) | Receptive, yin | Internal foundations and hidden capacities |
| 人道 (Human's Way) | Stem-branch interaction | Dynamic | The choices and actions that connect inner capacity to outer expression |

This framework means stems and branches are not simply additive. Stems show what a person projects; branches show what they have available. The interaction between them — 人道 — is where fate meets choice.
