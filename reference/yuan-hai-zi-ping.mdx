**Core contribution**: 四柱八字体系的奠基之作 — the foundational work of the Four Pillars system.

渊海子平 establishes the vocabulary and structural logic that all other classical texts build on: the ten-god (十神) system for labeling each stem and branch relative to the day master, the six-family (六亲) framework for reading relationships and people, the classification of chart patterns into regular and special types, and the four principles for selecting the useful god (用神).

<Info>
  Every other classical text in Bazi Skill's reference set assumes this text's framework. Ten-god labels, pattern names, and useful-god terminology all originate here.
</Info>

## Ten Gods and six-family relationships (十神与六亲)

The ten gods are generated by applying a fixed rule to the relationship between each stem or branch element and the day master's element, combined with yin/yang polarity matching. Each ten god carries both an elemental relationship and a set of human meanings.

The six-family (六亲) mappings define which people in the subject's life each ten god represents:

| Ten god (十神) | Relationship | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 正印 (Zhèng Yìn) — Regular Seal | Mother | Protective, nourishing authority |
| 偏财 (Piān Cái) — Indirect Wealth | Father (male chart) | Also: windfall wealth, paternal figures |
| 正财 (Zhèng Cái) — Regular Wealth | Wife / primary partner (male chart) | Steady, earned wealth |
| 正官 (Zhèng Guān) — Regular Officer | Husband / primary partner (female chart) | Also: employer, official authority |
| 食神 (Shí Shén) — Food God | Children (female chart); output, talent | Nurturing, generous expression |
| 伤官 (Shāng Guān) — Hurting Officer | Children (female chart); output, talent | More intense and unconventional than food god |
| 比肩 (Bǐ Jiān) — Parallel | Brothers, male peers (male chart) | Same element, same polarity as day master |
| 劫财 (Jié Cái) — Rob Wealth | Sisters, female peers (male chart) | Same element, opposite polarity |

<Note>
  The six-family mappings are written from the perspective of a male chart. In a female chart, the regular officer (正官) indicates the husband, and the food god or hurting officer indicates children. Bazi Skill adjusts these mappings automatically based on the subject's gender.
</Note>

## Pattern classification (格局分类)

### Regular patterns (正格)

Regular patterns are the eight standard configurations derived from the month branch's dominant element. Each is named after the ten god that governs the month branch.

| Pattern | Dominant ten god in month branch |
|---|---|
| 正官格 (Regular Officer) | 正官 |
| 偏官格 / 七杀格 (Seven Killings) | 七杀 / 偏官 |
| 正财格 (Regular Wealth) | 正财 |
| 偏财格 (Indirect Wealth) | 偏财 |
| 正印格 (Regular Seal) | 正印 |
| 偏印格 (Indirect Seal) | 偏印 |
| 食神格 (Food God) | 食神 |
| 伤官格 (Hurting Officer) | 伤官 |

For detailed rules governing each pattern's formation, breakage, and supporting configurations, see [三命通会](/reference/san-ming-tong-hui).

### Special patterns (特殊格)

Special patterns arise when the chart's elemental distribution is extreme enough that the regular pattern rules do not apply.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="从格 (Cóng Gé) — Following patterns">
    When the day master has no root (no branch support), no seasonal backing, and the chart is overwhelmingly dominated by one element or one type of ten god, the day master "follows" rather than resists.

    Following-pattern types:

    | Type | Follows | Condition |
    |---|---|---|
    | 从财格 | Wealth element | Day master rootless, wealth dominant throughout |
    | 从官杀格 | Officer/killings element | Day master rootless, officer or killings dominant |
    | 从儿格 | Food god / hurting officer | Day master rootless, output element dominant |
    | 从势格 | The strongest force overall | Multiple elements present but day master is without root or support |

    In a following pattern, the useful god is the dominant element — not the element that would conventionally "help" a weak day master. Supporting the day master against the dominant force breaks the pattern.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="化气格 (Huà Qì Gé) — Transformation patterns">
    When the day stem combines with an adjacent stem (天干五合) and both are supported by the month branch and the overall chart, the combination "transforms" into a new element. The resulting element becomes the day master's effective identity.

    The five stem combinations and their transformation elements:

    | Combination | Transforms to |
    |---|---|
    | 甲 + 己 | Earth (土) |
    | 乙 + 庚 | Metal (金) |
    | 丙 + 辛 | Water (水) |
    | 丁 + 壬 | Wood (木) |
    | 戊 + 癸 | Fire (火) |

    Transformation only occurs when the resulting element is supported by the month branch and no obstructing stems prevent the combination. A false transformation (假化格) — where the conditions are only partially met — produces a chart with unstable energy.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="建禄格 (Jiàn Lù Gé) — Established Prosperity pattern">
    When the month branch is at the 帝旺 (peak) position of the day master's element, the day master sits in its own season of maximum strength. 建禄格 indicates self-sufficiency and the ability to establish one's own path without relying on inherited resources.

    The useful god in 建禄格 is typically the officer, wealth, or food god — elements that productively deploy the day master's surplus strength.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="羊刃格 (Yáng Rèn Gé) — Goat Blade pattern">
    羊刃 is the branch where the day master's element is at its sharpest, most aggressive peak — slightly past 帝旺. The energy is forceful and cutting. Without proper control, the chart is unstable; with control (typically seven killings or regular officer), the energy becomes powerful and decisive.

    羊刃格 is associated with exceptional capability alongside a demanding temperament. Classical examples often cite military commanders and surgeons.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Four principles for choosing the useful god (用神取法)

The useful god (用神) is the element in the chart that most effectively resolves the chart's primary imbalance or unlocks its primary potential. 渊海子平 identifies four selection principles, applied in order of the chart's most urgent need.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. 扶抑 (Fú Yì) — Strengthen or restrain">
    The most common principle. Assess the day master's strength against the chart's overall elemental balance.

    - 身弱扶之 — if the day master is weak, strengthen it with seal (印) or parallel/rob-wealth (比劫).
    - 身旺抑之 — if the day master is strong, restrain it with wealth (财), officer/killings (官杀), or food god/hurting officer (食伤).

    This principle applies to the majority of conventional charts where the day master has a clear strength reading.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. 调候 (Tiáo Hòu) — Seasonal adjustment">
    When the seasonal imbalance is severe (extreme heat or cold), the seasonal adjustment god takes priority over the strength-based useful god. Even a strong day master that is "overcooking" in summer heat needs water before anything else.

    For the full seasonal adjustment god prescriptions, see [穷通宝典](/reference/qiong-tong-bao-dian).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. 通关 (Tōng Guān) — Mediation">
    When two major elements are locked in direct conflict and neither can dominate, the useful god is the element that mediates between them — not one of the combatants.

    Example: if Wood and Metal are evenly matched and in direct confrontation, Water is the mediation useful god (Metal generates Water; Water generates Wood — the confrontation becomes a generation chain).

    For the full mediation element table, see [滴天髓](/reference/di-tian-sui).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. 顺势 (Shùn Shì) — Follow the force">
    In following patterns (从格), the useful god is the dominant force itself. The correct move is to follow the chart's strongest current, not resist it.

    Introducing the day master's supporting elements in a true following pattern "breaks the follow" and produces instability rather than improvement.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
