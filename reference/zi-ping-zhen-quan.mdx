**Core contribution**: 格局论命的集大成之作，用神专论 — the definitive synthesis of pattern-based fate reading and the authoritative treatment of useful god theory.

While 渊海子平 establishes the system and 三命通会 catalogs its applications, 子平真诠 refines the analytical framework into a precise quality assessment. It defines what makes a pattern excellent or poor, introduces the four-god hierarchy (useful, supporting, favorable, opposing), and articulates a gradient from complete success to complete failure. Bazi Skill uses this text to translate chart structure into outcome quality.

## Pattern quality: clarity and murkiness

The text's central evaluative axis is 清纯 versus 杂浊 — clarity versus murkiness.

> 格局以清纯为贵，杂浊为贱。
>
> *Patterns are distinguished by clarity; they are diminished by murkiness.*

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="清纯 (Qīng Chún) — Clarity">
    A clear pattern has one coherent useful god, that useful god is unattacked, and the chart's structure consistently supports it.

    **Markers of clarity**:
    - A single ten god governs the month branch and appears cleanly on the stems without competition from other ten gods of the same or conflicting type.
    - The useful god occupies a position of strength and is not clashed, combined away, or buried by opposing elements.
    - Supporting elements reinforce rather than complicate the useful god's role.

    Clarity correlates with a life that moves along a consistent, recognizable trajectory. Talent and effort lead to proportionate outcomes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="杂浊 (Zá Zhuó) — Murkiness">
    A murky pattern has multiple competing useful gods, or the useful god is under attack from multiple directions, or the chart's structure sends contradictory signals.

    **Markers of murkiness**:
    - Multiple ten gods appear transparently on the stems, each claiming pattern authority.
    - The useful god is simultaneously supported and attacked, leaving its net strength ambiguous.
    - The chart's elemental configuration shifts depending on which branch combination is activated.

    Murkiness correlates with a life of contradiction: capability that is inconsistently expressed, opportunities that arrive alongside their own obstacles, outcomes that do not match effort.

    <Note>
      Murkiness is not the same as weakness. A murky chart can be powerful. Its problem is instability and inconsistency, not lack of capacity.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Pattern formation and breaking conditions

### Formation conditions (成格条件)

A pattern is considered well-formed when all three conditions are met:

1. **用神有力** — the useful god is strong. It occupies a favorable seasonal position or has strong branch roots.
2. **不受克破** — the useful god is not attacked. No opposing element is strong enough to damage or neutralize it.
3. **有相神辅助** — a supporting god (相神) is present and reinforces the useful god's function.

### Breaking conditions (破格条件)

A pattern breaks when any of the following conditions apply:

1. **用神受克** — the useful god is directly attacked by a strong opposing element.
2. **忌神当令无制** — the opposing god (忌神) is in seasonal command and has no controlling element to neutralize it.
3. **格局混杂** — the pattern is contaminated by multiple conflicting structural claims, none of which dominates cleanly.

<Warning>
  Pattern breaking is not always permanent. A broken pattern can be repaired by a luck cycle (大运) that removes the offending element, restores the useful god's strength, or introduces the missing supporting god. Reading the luck cycles against the chart structure is where 子平真诠's framework is most practically applied.
</Warning>

## The four-god hierarchy

子平真诠 introduces precise terminology for the four roles that ten gods play in relation to the pattern:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="用神 (Yòng Shén) — Useful god">
    The most critical ten god in the chart: the one that maintains the pattern's structural balance.

    In a 正官格 (Regular Officer pattern), the regular officer itself is the useful god. In a 七杀格 (Seven Killings pattern), the food god that controls the killings is the useful god.

    The useful god's condition — its strength, position, and whether it is protected or attacked — is the single most important factor in assessing chart quality and life outcomes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="相神 (Xiāng Shén) — Supporting god">
    The ten god that directly reinforces the useful god's function.

    **Classic example**: in a 正官格, the useful god is the regular officer (正官). If a seal (印) is also present, it receives the officer's generation and protects the day master — this is 官印相生, and the seal is the 相神.

    The supporting god amplifies the useful god's positive contribution. A pattern with both a strong useful god and a strong supporting god is more robust than one with a useful god alone.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="喜神 (Xǐ Shén) — Favorable god">
    The element that generates or protects the useful god, at one step of remove.

    Where the 相神 directly assists the useful god, the 喜神 assists the 相神 or creates conditions favorable to the useful god's health.

    **Example**: if the useful god is the regular officer and the supporting god is the seal, a water element that generates the seal is the 喜神 — it feeds the support chain.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="忌神 (Jì Shén) — Opposing god">
    The element that attacks, neutralizes, or destabilizes the useful god.

    An 忌神 in seasonal command (得令) without a controlling element is the primary marker of a broken pattern. Luck cycles that strengthen the 忌神 are the most damaging periods in the chart's timeline; luck cycles that control or remove the 忌神 are among the most favorable.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Success and failure gradient

子平真诠 does not treat pattern quality as binary. It maps chart outcomes onto a gradient from complete success to complete failure, with two intermediate states that describe the majority of real charts.

| Outcome | Conditions | Life quality |
|---|---|---|
| 成格 — Complete success | Useful god strong and protected; supporting god present and active; opposing god controlled or absent | Sustained achievement; talent and effort produce proportionate outcomes; major luck cycles reinforce rather than disrupt |
| 成中有败 — Success with partial failure | Pattern well-formed but has a specific structural flaw: useful god slightly attacked, supporting god present but weak, or opposing god present but controlled | Generally successful life with identifiable recurring obstacles; the flaw's nature predicts the obstacle's domain |
| 败中有成 — Failure with partial success | Pattern structurally weak but has a redeeming feature: opposing god dominant but one luck cycle neutralizes it, or useful god absent in the chart but arrives in a critical luck period | Life of significant difficulty with notable periods of achievement; success is episodic rather than sustained |
| 败格 — Complete failure | Useful god attacked and unable to function; opposing god in seasonal command with no control; no supporting god present | Persistent obstruction; the chart's structural potential cannot express; improvement depends almost entirely on external luck cycle support |

<Tip>
  In practice, 成中有败 and 败中有成 are the most common outcomes. The task is to identify the specific nature of the flaw or redeeming feature, because that precision is what makes the reading actionable: naming the obstacle and locating when the redeeming element arrives in the luck cycles gives the subject something concrete to work with.
</Tip>

## How Bazi Skill uses this text

子平真诠 is applied at the synthesis stage of every reading, after seasonal adjustment (穷通宝典), strength assessment (滴天髓), and pattern identification (三命通会, 渊海子平) are complete.

The skill uses the text's framework to:

1. **Assess pattern clarity**: is the useful god singular and uncontested, or does the chart have competing structural claims?
2. **Assign god roles**: label each relevant ten god as useful god, supporting god, favorable god, or opposing god.
3. **Place the chart on the success-failure gradient**: determine which of the four outcome states the chart's structure most closely resembles.
4. **Map the luck cycles**: identify which major luck periods (大运) strengthen the useful god, reinforce the supporting god, or neutralize the opposing god — and which do the reverse.
