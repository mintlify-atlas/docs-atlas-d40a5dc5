## Early and late Zi-hour (早子时 vs 夜子时)

Zi-hour (子时) spans midnight and therefore crosses two calendar days (23:00–01:00). Classical astrology distinguishes two sub-periods:

- **夜子时 Late Zi-hour** (23:00–24:00): belongs to the **next** day — use the next day's day-pillar when deriving the hour pillar.
- **早子时 Early Zi-hour** (00:00–01:00): belongs to the **current** day — use the current day's day-pillar.

<Note>
The assignment of Zi-hour to a calendar day is a long-standing debate in classical astrology. This skill uses the early/late Zi-hour distinction: births from 23:00 onward are computed against the next day's day stem.
</Note>

## Five-Rat Escaping Method (五鼠遁元)

The heavenly stem of the hour pillar is determined by the day master's stem. The mnemonic verse is:

> **甲己还加甲，乙庚丙作初，丙辛从戊起，丁壬庚子居，戊癸何方发，壬子是真途。**

This means: when the day stem is 甲 or 己, Zi-hour begins with 甲 (甲子时); when 乙 or 庚, with 丙 (丙子时); and so on.

<Tip>
Each row cycles the ten heavenly stems forward starting from the Zi-hour stem given by the mnemonic. You do not need to memorize the full table — just remember the five starting stems (甲, 丙, 戊, 庚, 壬) and count forward.
</Tip>
