**Core contribution**: 调候用神 — seasonal adjustment gods.

For each combination of day master (日干) and birth month (月令), the text prescribes the specific element(s) that balance the seasonal climate of the chart. This is the most direct and actionable prescription in the classical canon: not a general principle but a table of specific remedies.

<Tip>
  Bazi Skill applies 调候用神 first in every reading, before assessing pattern (格局) or strength (旺衰). If the adjustment god is absent or suppressed, obstacles arise regardless of how well-formed the pattern is.
</Tip>

## The key principle

> 论命首重调候，其次才论格局旺衰。若命局缺调候之字，格局再好也难发挥。
>
> *Fate reading prioritizes seasonal adjustment first, then pattern and strength. If the chart lacks the adjustment-god character, even an excellent pattern cannot fully manifest.*

The logic is climatic: a day master born into extreme heat or extreme cold needs a counterbalancing element before anything else can function. Pattern analysis assumes a livable climate; adjustment god analysis establishes whether that climate exists.

## Summer and winter rules

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Summer (夏令) — fire dominant, water required">
    When a chart is born in summer months (巳、午、未 — Months 4, 5, 6), fire (火) is at peak strength. The chart overheats.

    **General rule**: 夏生火旺，须用水调候 — born in summer with fire dominant, use water as the adjustment god.

    Water cools the chart and allows other elements to function. Without water, even a well-structured pattern produces outcomes that are strained, hot-tempered, or unsustainable.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Winter (冬令) — water dominant, fire required">
    When a chart is born in winter months (亥、子、丑 — Months 10, 11, 12), water (水) is at peak strength. The chart freezes.

    **General rule**: 冬生水旺，须用火调候 — born in winter with water dominant, use fire as the adjustment god.

    Fire warms the chart and activates dormant elements. Without fire, the chart's potential remains locked in cold stasis.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Key examples from the text

The text specifies adjustment gods for each of the ten day masters across all twelve months. The following three examples illustrate the precision of the system.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="甲木 born in Month 1 (寅月, early spring)">
    **Situation**: Early spring — the cold of winter has not fully lifted. 甲木 (Jiǎ Wood, the eldest yang wood) is in its home branch (寅 contains 甲), so the day master has root. But residual cold suppresses growth.

    **Prescription**:
    1. First use 丙火 (Bǐng Fire) to warm the wood and drive away the remaining cold.
    2. Then use 癸水 (Guǐ Water) to moisten and nourish the wood's growth.

    **Outcome**: If both 丙 and 癸 appear transparently on the heavenly stems (两透), the chart achieves both wealth and honor (富贵双全).

    **Implication for reading**: A Month-1 甲木 chart missing 丙 on the stems has a cold climate problem — strong pattern notwithstanding, the person faces persistent effort-without-reward dynamics until a luck cycle brings 丙.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="甲木 born in Month 8 (酉月, autumn metal dominant)">
    **Situation**: Autumn — metal (金) is at peak power. 酉 is the roost of 辛金. 甲木 is under direct attack from the seasonal metal force.

    **Prescription**:
    1. First use 丁火 (Dīng Fire) to restrain the metal (制金), protecting the wood day master.
    2. Then use 丙火 (Bǐng Fire) to warm and support the wood.

    **Implication for reading**: Without 丁 in the chart, the metal attack on 甲木 is uncontrolled. 丙 alone warms but does not restrain. The adjustment sequence matters: control the threat before applying nourishment.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="庚金 born in Month 11 (子月, deep winter)">
    **Situation**: Deep winter — water (水) is at maximum strength in 子月. 庚金 (Gēng Metal, yang metal) generates water but is itself frozen. Metal sinks in cold water; it cannot function.

    **Prescription**:
    1. 丙火 (Bǐng Fire) is essential to thaw the metal and activate the chart.
    2. 甲木 (Jiǎ Wood) feeds 丁火 (Dīng Fire), which in turn smelts 庚金 and brings it to useful form.

    > 寒冬水冷金沉，必用丙火解冻，甲木引丁。
    >
    > *In cold winter with frozen water and sunken metal, 丙 fire must thaw the freeze; 甲 wood feeds 丁 fire.*

    **Implication for reading**: A winter 庚金 chart without 丙 cannot activate its potential regardless of pattern quality. The "sunken metal" metaphor is literal: 庚金's cutting, structuring force is inoperative until warmed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How Bazi Skill uses this text

The skill follows the text's own stated priority: adjustment god analysis precedes all other analysis.

The reading checks two things about the adjustment god:

1. **Presence**: Is the prescribed adjustment god character (the specific stem or branch) present in the chart?
2. **Strength**: Is it protected — not clashed, combined away, or buried under opposing elements?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Adjustment god present and strong">
    The climate is livable. The reading proceeds to pattern and strength analysis. The adjustment god's presence is noted as a foundational strength of the chart.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Adjustment god present but weak or attacked">
    The climate is marginal. The reading notes that the chart's potential is partially suppressed. Luck cycles (大运) that strengthen or protect the adjustment god represent periods of significant improvement.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Adjustment god absent">
    The climate is hostile. Even a well-formed pattern operates under persistent constraint. The reading identifies which luck cycles bring the adjustment god and frames those as the person's primary windows for achievement.

    <Warning>
      Absence of the adjustment god does not mean the chart is "bad" — it means the person faces a specific, identifiable obstacle. Naming the obstacle and its remedy is more useful than a global quality judgment.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
