Bazi Skill grounds every reading in the classical Chinese Four Pillars (八字) literature rather than synthesized summaries. Each text contributes a distinct analytical lens — seasonal adjustment, strength assessment, pattern formation, or critical analysis — and the skill applies them in a defined hierarchy: seasonal adjustment (调候) first, then strength (旺衰), then pattern (格局).

The nine texts below represent the core canon. Five of them receive dedicated reference pages because they contribute the most directly to the analytical pipeline; the other four are summarized in the table at the bottom of this page.

<Note>
  Classical texts are used as analytical authority, not as fixed lookup tables. Bazi Skill synthesizes multiple texts when they address the same chart feature, and notes where classical sources diverge.
</Note>

## All nine texts at a glance

| Text (Chinese · Pinyin) | Core contribution | Skill usage context |
|---|---|---|
| 穷通宝典 · *Qiong Tong Bao Dian* | Seasonal adjustment gods (调候用神) | First check in every reading: is the adjustment god present and strong? |
| 三命通会 · *San Ming Tong Hui* | Patterns and divine stars (格局神煞) | Pattern identification and star interpretation throughout the reading |
| 滴天髓 · *Di Tian Sui* | Strength assessment and Qi flow (五行旺衰) | Judges day master strength via 得令, 得地, 得势; evaluates flow blockages |
| 渊海子平 · *Yuan Hai Zi Ping* | Ten Gods and six-family relationships (十神六亲) | Foundational system for all Ten God labeling and family/relationship readings |
| 千里命稿 · *Qian Li Ming Gao* | Practical case analysis (命例实证) | Guides pillar-by-pillar timing analysis and event prediction methodology |
| 协纪辨方书 · *Xie Ji Bian Fang Shu* | Date selection and divine stars (择日神煞) | Source for divine star derivation rules (year stem / year branch lookups) |
| 果老星宗 · *Guo Lao Xing Zong* | Star astrology cross-reference (星命合参) | Auxiliary reference only; life-palace (命宫) calculation when needed |
| 子平真诠 · *Zi Ping Zhen Quan* | Pattern-based destiny (用神格局) | Defines pattern quality (clear vs. murky) and success/failure gradients |
| 神峰通考 · *Shen Feng Tong Kao* | Critical analysis and correcting errors (命理辨误) | Invoked when mechanical pattern application would produce incorrect results |

## Texts covered on this page only

The four texts below do not have dedicated pages. Their contributions are narrow enough to be applied directly during the reading without a separate deep-dive reference.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="千里命稿 (Qian Li Ming Gao) — Practical case analysis">
    **Core contribution**: concrete断命 (fate-reading) technique drawn from real chart examples.

    The text establishes a pillar-by-pillar timing framework:

    | Pillar | Represents | Life stage |
    |---|---|---|
    | Year pillar (年柱) | Ancestors, family background | Ages 1–16 |
    | Month pillar (月柱) | Parents, siblings, work environment | Ages 17–32 |
    | Day pillar (日柱) | Self (day stem), spouse (day branch) | Ages 33–48 |
    | Hour pillar (时柱) | Children, late life, career outcomes | Age 49 onward |

    The skill uses this framework when timing life events: first assess pattern quality, then check whether the major luck cycle (大运) supports or opposes the useful god, then check whether the annual cycle (流年) activates a critical position.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="协纪辨方书 (Xie Ji Bian Fang Shu) — Date selection and divine stars">
    **Core contribution**: systematic derivation rules for divine stars (神煞), sourced from year stem and year branch.

    Auspicious stars covered: 天乙贵人 (Heavenly Yi), 天德贵人 (Heavenly Virtue), 月德贵人 (Monthly Virtue), 文昌 (Literary Flourishing), 学堂 (Scholar Hall).

    Inauspicious stars covered: 羊刃 (Goat Blade), 亡神 (Death Spirit), 劫煞 (Robbery Sha), 灾煞 (Disaster Sha), 六厄 (Six Adversities), 孤辰 (Lonely Pillar), 寡宿 (Widow Lodge).

    <Warning>
      Divine stars are auxiliary reference only. They must be read alongside pattern strength and useful god status. An auspicious star is weakened when attacked; an inauspicious star is neutralized when properly controlled.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="果老星宗 (Guo Lao Xing Zong) — Star astrology cross-reference">
    **Core contribution**: a separate star-based astrology system (星命术) that overlaps partially with Four Pillars.

    The text uses the life palace (命宫) and body palace (身宫) as its primary axes, interpreted through the Seven Governors and Four Remainders (七政四余): sun, moon, Venus, Jupiter, Mercury, Mars, Saturn, plus Rahu, Ketu, comet, and vapour.

    Some concepts have been absorbed into Four Pillars practice, particularly life-palace calculation:

    > Start from the Yin branch (寅) at the birth month, count forward to the birth hour — the branch where you land is the life palace (命宫).

    <Info>
      Star astrology content is auxiliary in Bazi Skill readings. The life-palace calculation is included when it adds meaningful interpretive context; it is never required.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="神峰通考 (Shen Feng Tong Kao) — Critical analysis">
    **Core contribution**: methodological critique of mechanical pattern application; the "illness and medicine" (病药) doctrine.

    Key arguments from the text:

    - A chart without a flaw (病, literally "illness") is unremarkable. A chart that has a clear flaw and also has a correcting element (药, "medicine") in the luck cycles is what produces achievement.
    - Divine stars should not be applied mechanically. Five-element generation and restraint is the root; stars are secondary.
    - Pattern rules have fixed forms, but flexible interpretation (变通) is more important than rigid adherence.

    The skill invokes this text as a check against over-literal pattern application, particularly when a classically "broken" pattern produces a chart that classical examples show as successful.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
