**Core contribution**: 格局论命、神煞体系 — pattern-based fate reading and the divine stars system.

三命通会 is the most encyclopedic of the classical texts. It codifies the rules for each of the eight standard patterns, catalogs hundreds of divine stars with their derivation mnemonics, and documents stem-branch relationships in exhaustive detail. Bazi Skill draws on it throughout a reading: for pattern identification, for star interpretation, and as a cross-reference for edge cases.

## Core patterns (核心格局)

A pattern (格局) is established when the dominant element in the month branch (月支) appears transparently on the heavenly stems. The month branch is the strongest positional influence in the chart; whatever element governs it defines the chart's structural identity.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="正官格 (Zhèng Guān Gé) — Regular Officer pattern">
    **Formation**: the month branch contains the regular officer (正官) of the day master, and it appears on the stems.

    **Character**: the person is upright, dignified, and possesses natural authority. The pattern is associated with official positions and social recognition.

    **Key rule**: 忌伤官破格 — the pattern is broken when the Hurting Officer (伤官) appears and attacks the regular officer. Injury to the officer destroys the chart's structural integrity.

    **Supporting element**: an印星 (seal/印) alongside the officer creates the classic 官印相生 (officer-seal mutual generation) formation, which elevates the pattern's quality.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="七杀格 (Qī Shā Gé) — Seven Killings pattern">
    **Formation**: the month branch contains the seven killings (七杀, also called 偏官), and it appears on the stems.

    **Character**: the person is forceful, decisive, and capable of handling difficult situations. The raw energy of seven killings is powerful but unstable.

    **Key rules**:
    - 须食神制杀 — the Food God (食神) must control the killings. Uncontrolled seven killings create aggression and conflict.
    - 须印化杀 — alternatively, a seal (印) can transform the killings' energy into authority. This is the "seal transforms killings" (印化杀) configuration.

    Without either control mechanism, seven killings remain dangerous energy.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="正财格 / 偏财格 (Zhèng Cái / Piān Cái Gé) — Wealth patterns">
    **Formation**: the month branch contains the regular wealth (正财) or indirect wealth (偏财) star of the day master, appearing on the stems.

    **Character**: 正财 (regular wealth) indicates careful, steady wealth accumulation. 偏财 (indirect wealth) indicates bold, entrepreneurial wealth — and often a generous nature.

    **Key rule**: 忌比劫争财 — the pattern is damaged when Parallel (比肩) or Rob Wealth (劫财) siblings appear and compete for the wealth star. Wealth shared among many siblings diminishes for each.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="正印格 / 偏印格 (Zhèng Yìn / Piān Yìn Gé) — Seal patterns">
    **Formation**: the month branch contains the regular seal (正印) or indirect seal (偏印), appearing on the stems.

    **Character**: 正印 indicates intelligence, learning, and maternal support. 偏印 also supports the day master but does so in a less stable, more specialized way.

    **Key rule**: 偏印忌见食神（枭神夺食）— the indirect seal must not encounter the food god. When 偏印 (also called 枭神, "owl spirit") attacks the food god, it destroys the chart's nurturing outlet. This configuration — 枭神夺食, "owl robs food" — is one of the classical chart-damage patterns.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="食神格 (Shí Shén Gé) — Food God pattern">
    **Formation**: the month branch contains the food god (食神), appearing on the stems.

    **Character**: abundance, talent, a comfortable life, and artistic or creative aptitude. The food god represents the day master's generative output flowing smoothly.

    **Key rule**: 忌枭印夺食 — the indirect seal (枭神/偏印) must not attack the food god. The food god's abundance is its defining quality; losing it to an indirect seal hollows the pattern.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="伤官格 (Shāng Guān Gé) — Hurting Officer pattern">
    **Formation**: the month branch contains the hurting officer (伤官), appearing on the stems.

    **Character**: exceptional talent, unconventional intelligence, and an independent spirit. Hurting officer types often excel in creative, technical, or artistic fields.

    **Key rule**: 伤官见官为祸百端 — when the hurting officer encounters the regular officer (正官), disasters multiply. The hurting officer's nature is to damage authority; placing it alongside the officer it damages invites conflict and loss of status.

    <Note>
      The rule 伤官见官 has well-documented exceptions in the classical literature. Some seasonal configurations (particularly metal hurting officer in winter, or wood hurting officer in spring) can tolerate or even benefit from the presence of the officer. The rule is a strong default, not an absolute.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common divine stars (常用神煞)

Divine stars are secondary analytical markers. They amplify or color the reading but do not override pattern and useful-god analysis. An auspicious star loses power when attacked; an inauspicious star is neutralized when controlled.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="天乙贵人 (Tiān Yǐ Guì Rén) — Heavenly Yi Noble">
    **Meaning**: the most auspicious divine star. Indicates the ability to receive help in times of crisis, turn misfortune into fortune, and attract powerful benefactors.

    **Mnemonic** (derived from the day stem):

    > 甲戊庚牛羊，乙己鼠猴乡，丙丁猪鸡位，壬癸兔蛇藏，庚辛逢虎马
    >
    > - 甲, 戊, 庚 day stems → 贵人 at 丑 (ox) and 未 (goat)
    > - 乙, 己 day stems → 贵人 at 子 (rat) and 申 (monkey)
    > - 丙, 丁 day stems → 贵人 at 亥 (pig) and 酉 (rooster)
    > - 壬, 癸 day stems → 贵人 at 卯 (rabbit) and 巳 (snake)
    > - 庚, 辛 day stems → 贵人 at 寅 (tiger) and 午 (horse)

    The 贵人 branch appearing in any of the four pillars (or in a luck cycle) activates the star's protective function.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="文昌贵人 (Wén Chāng Guì Rén) — Literary Flourishing Noble">
    **Meaning**: intelligence, scholarly aptitude, eloquence, and distinction in written or artistic pursuits. Associated with academic success and examination results in the classical era; in modern readings, creative and intellectual achievement.

    The star is derived from the day stem and represents the channel through which the day master's mental energy flows most freely.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="驿马 (Yì Mǎ) — Traveling Horse">
    **Meaning**: movement, travel, relocation, and career transitions. The horse is always in motion. Its presence in a chart indicates a life of change and movement; its activation by a luck cycle or annual pillar triggers literal or figurative travel.

    **Branch-based lookup** (derived from the year branch or day branch):

    | Branch group | 驿马 location |
    |---|---|
    | 申、子、辰 (water frame) | 寅 (Tiger) |
    | 寅、午、戌 (fire frame) | 申 (Monkey) |
    | 巳、酉、丑 (metal frame) | 亥 (Pig) |
    | 亥、卯、未 (wood frame) | 巳 (Snake) |

    A 驿马 that is clashed (冲) by another branch is "in motion" — the movement it represents is activated, often suddenly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="桃花 (Táo Huā) — Peach Blossom">
    **Meaning**: romantic attraction, personal magnetism, artistic talent, and social charm. Also called 咸池 (Xián Chí). Its presence in the chart indicates strong interpersonal appeal; excessive or uncontrolled 桃花 can indicate romantic instability.

    **Branch-based lookup**:

    | Branch group | 桃花 location |
    |---|---|
    | 申、子、辰 | 酉 (Rooster) |
    | 寅、午、戌 | 卯 (Rabbit) |
    | 巳、酉、丑 | 午 (Horse) |
    | 亥、卯、未 | 子 (Rat) |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="华盖 (Huá Gài) — Scholar's Cover">
    **Meaning**: solitude, spiritual depth, academic distinction, and religious or philosophical inclination. The华盖 is the canopy over the emperor's carriage — it marks the person as singular, set apart. In practice it often correlates with independent scholarly or spiritual paths, and sometimes with isolation.

    **Branch-based lookup**:

    | Branch group | 华盖 location |
    |---|---|
    | 申、子、辰 | 辰 (Dragon) |
    | 寅、午、戌 | 戌 (Dog) |
    | 巳、酉、丑 | 丑 (Ox) |
    | 亥、卯、未 | 未 (Goat) |

    <Info>
      华盖 sitting on the day branch or hour branch is particularly significant. On the day branch it colors the person's inner character and private life; on the hour branch it often indicates a solitary or unconventional path in later life.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
