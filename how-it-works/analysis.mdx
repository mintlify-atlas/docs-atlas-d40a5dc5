---
title: "Analysis"
description: "How the Bazi skill interprets a completed chart across eight structured analysis components."
---

Phase 3 (第三阶段：综合分析) applies classical theory to the chart produced in Phase 2. Before writing the analysis, the skill consults its classical-texts reference to draw on the nine canonical texts. Every conclusion references its source text so you can trace the reasoning.

<Info>
  The nine classical texts consulted are: 《穷通宝典》《三命通会》《滴天髓》《渊海子平》《千里命稿》《协纪辨方书》《子平真诠》《神峰通考》《果老星宗》. Source citations appear inline throughout the analysis.
</Info>

The eight analysis components run in sequence:

<Steps>

  <Step title="Day-master analysis (日主分析)">
    The **day stem (日主)** is the self — the reference point for the entire chart. This section determines how strong or weak that self is within the chart's balance of forces.

    Strength is assessed across three dimensions, following 《滴天髓》:

    - **得令 (seasonal authority)** — Does the day stem's element correspond to the month branch's dominant qi? This is the most important factor. A wood day master born in spring (寅卯月) is 得令; one born in autumn (申酉月) is not.
    - **得地 (rooted in branches)** — Does the day stem have roots (长生, 帝旺, or 墓库 positions) in any of the four branches? Roots in the day or hour branch are especially meaningful.
    - **得势 (supported by stems)** — Are there fellow-element stems (比肩/劫财) or resource stems (印星) in the chart that reinforce the day master?

    <Tip>
      Of the three, 得令 carries the greatest weight. A day master that holds seasonal authority can often compensate for weak roots or limited stem support.
    </Tip>

    From this assessment, the day master is classified as one of four strengths:

    | Classification | Description |
    |---|---|
    | 身旺 (strong) | Day master is well-supported; benefits from restraint or output |
    | 身弱 (weak) | Day master is undermined; benefits from resource and peer support |
    | 从强 (following-strong) | Day master is overwhelmingly outmatched; follows the dominant force |
    | 从弱 (following-weak) | Day master is so weakly supported it conforms entirely to the opposition |

    The section also covers how the day master's element shapes temperament and behavior — for example, 甲木 (yang wood) tends toward ambition and upward momentum, while 癸水 (yin water) tends toward adaptability and perceptiveness.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ten gods analysis (十神分析)">
    The ten gods (十神) classify every other stem in the chart by its relationship to the day master. This section identifies which ten gods are present, where they sit (year, month, hour pillar or in hidden stems), and how they interact with the day master.

    The six-family relationships drawn from 《渊海子平》 are:

    | Ten god | Family relationship |
    |---|---|
    | 正印 | Mother |
    | 偏财 | Father (male chart) |
    | 正财 | Wife (male chart) / stable income |
    | 正官 | Husband (female chart) / official authority |
    | 食神 / 伤官 | Children (female chart) / talent and expression |
    | 比肩 | Brothers / peers |

    The analysis highlights the **most influential** ten gods — those that appear in prominent positions (especially the month pillar), are transparent in the stems, or form combinations with the day master.

    <Info>
      Pillar positions correspond to life stages per 《千里命稿》: year pillar → ancestry and childhood (ages 1–16); month pillar → parents, siblings, and early career (17–32); day pillar → self and spouse (33–48); hour pillar → children and later years (49+).
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Five-element balance (五行平衡)">
    This section maps the distribution of the five elements (木火土金水) across the full chart — stems, branches, and hidden stems — and identifies which elements dominate and which are absent or weak.

    From this distribution, the skill determines:

    - **喜用神 (favorable gods)** — elements and ten gods the day master needs. For a weak day master, this typically means resource stars (印星) and peer stars (比劫). For a strong day master, it means output (食伤) and restraint (财官).
    - **忌神 (unfavorable gods)** — elements and ten gods that harm the chart's balance.

    <Tip>
      The skill applies 《穷通宝典》's **seasonal adjustment (调候)** principle on top of the strength-based analysis. Even a structurally sound chart may need a specific element for temperature or moisture balance — for example, a 甲木 day master born in the height of summer (午月) urgently needs 癸水 to moderate the heat, regardless of overall strength. This "medicinal" element takes priority.
    </Tip>

    The output includes a short table of the five-element counts and a plain-language statement of which elements to welcome and which to avoid in luck cycles and the annual cycle.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pattern determination (格局判定)">
    A chart's pattern (格局) is its structural archetype, determined by the **month branch's dominant hidden stem** and whether that qi is transparent in the stems.

    The eight standard patterns from 《子平真诠》:

    | Pattern | Basis |
    |---|---|
    | 正官格 | Month branch hides and reveals 正官 |
    | 偏官格 (七杀格) | Month branch hides and reveals 偏官 |
    | 正财格 | Month branch hides and reveals 正财 |
    | 偏财格 | Month branch hides and reveals 偏财 |
    | 正印格 | Month branch hides and reveals 正印 |
    | 偏印格 | Month branch hides and reveals 偏印 |
    | 食神格 | Month branch hides and reveals 食神 |
    | 伤官格 | Month branch hides and reveals 伤官 |

    Special patterns — from格 (following patterns), 化气格, 建禄格, 羊刃格 — are assessed when the standard pattern conditions are not met.

    <Info>
      Per 《子平真诠》, a pattern's quality is judged by **clarity (清纯)**. A single, well-supported pattern with a coherent use-god and a complementary supporting-god (相神) is a high-quality pattern. Multiple competing pattern elements, or a use-god that is clashed or blocked, degrades the pattern.
    </Info>

    The analysis states whether the pattern is **成格 (formed)** or **破格 (broken)** and explains the key factor tipping it one way or the other.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Major luck cycle analysis (大运分析)">
    Each ten-year major luck cycle interacts with the original chart like a changing environment. This section first identifies **which cycle you are currently in**, then surveys the full sequence.

    For each cycle, the skill evaluates:

    - Whether the cycle stem and branch favor or oppose the use-god (喜神 arriving → auspicious; 忌神 arriving → inauspicious).
    - Structural interactions: heavenly stem harmony/clash (天合/天克) and earthly branch harmony/clash/punishment (地合/地冲/地刑).
    - The transition year (换运年), which typically brings notable change as one cycle's influence wanes and the next rises.

    <Tip>
      One classical school holds that the heavenly stem of a cycle governs the first five years while the earthly branch governs the latter five — though many practitioners apply both stem and branch throughout the full ten years. The analysis notes when stem and branch pull in opposing directions within a single cycle.
    </Tip>

    The current cycle receives the most detailed treatment, including how it modifies your day-master's strength and how it interacts with the pattern.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Annual luck analysis (流年分析)">
    The annual luck (流年) is the heavenly stem and earthly branch of the current calendar year. It acts as the immediate trigger within the environment set by the major cycle.

    The analysis covers:

    - The **current year's** stem-branch and its relationship to both the original chart and the active major cycle.
    - A directional forecast for the **next 1–3 years**, noting upcoming annual stems/branches that are particularly aligned with or contrary to your use-god.

    Interaction principles for reading luck cycle combinations:

    | Combination | Effect |
    |---|---|
    | Strong cycle + favorable annual | Peak opportunity |
    | Strong cycle + unfavorable annual | Generally smooth with a single-year obstacle |
    | Weak cycle + favorable annual | Brief opening within a difficult period |
    | Weak cycle + unfavorable annual | Compound difficulty; exercise caution |

    <Note>
      Annual luck analysis for a deceased subject stops at the year of death. No projections are made beyond that year.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Historical calibration (历史事件校准)">
    This is a critical verification step. Before finalizing the analysis, the skill generates **3–5 retrospective predictions** — specific time windows and event types that the chart logic suggests should have been significant — and asks you to confirm whether they match your actual experience.

    Example prompts:

    - "根据您XX岁（XXXX年）的大运流年组合，那一年应该有XXXX方面的重大变化（如事业转折/感情变化/搬迁等），是否准确？"
    - "您XX–XX岁这步大运整体呈现XXXX趋势，那个阶段您的生活重心应该在XXXX方面。"

    Your feedback directly adjusts the analysis model. If multiple retrospective predictions are confirmed, the skill proceeds with confidence. If key predictions miss, it revisits the pattern and use-god assessment — for example, reconsidering whether the chart is a standard pattern or a special following-pattern — and revises accordingly.

    <Info>
      This calibration step reflects the 《神峰通考》 principle of "病药说" (illness and remedy): a correct diagnosis is confirmed when the prescribed "remedy" (喜用神 arriving in a cycle) maps accurately onto positive real-world outcomes. Misalignment signals a diagnostic error, not a flaw in the classical system.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Comprehensive recommendations (综合建议)">
    The final section synthesizes all preceding analysis into actionable guidance across four domains:

    **Career and direction (事业方向)**
    Identifies fields and working styles that align with your use-god's element. For example, a chart that favors 木 may suit roles in growth-oriented industries, education, or planning.

    **Financial trends (财运趋势)**
    Describes periods when wealth stars are active and periods to exercise caution. Notes whether your chart structure supports steady accumulation, entrepreneurial risk, or inheritance-type wealth.

    **Relationships and marriage (感情婚姻)**
    Interprets the spouse-star (正财 for men, 正官 for women) in terms of timing and quality. Notes any chart features that affect relationship stability.

    **Health considerations (健康注意事项)**
    Maps each element to the organ systems it governs and flags any elements that are severely over- or under-represented. For example, excessive 金 can indicate respiratory or skin concerns; insufficient 水 may relate to kidney or adrenal issues.

    Optional supplementary guidance (吉祥方位、颜色等) — auspicious directions, colors, and materials aligned with the use-god's element — may be included at the end.

    <Warning>
      The skill closes every analysis with the reminder: "命理分析仅供参考，人生在于自身的努力和选择。" All health-related observations are informational only; consult a medical professional for any health concerns. Financial observations are for cultural reference; make financial decisions based on your own informed judgment.
    </Warning>

    Every recommendation cites the classical source behind it — for example: "根据《穷通宝典》的调候原则，甲木生于酉月，先用丁火制金……"
  </Step>

</Steps>
